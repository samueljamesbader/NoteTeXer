About

Note$\TeX$er

Create a knowledge web in seconds.
Speedtype $\LaTeX$, even by phone.
Share with classmates.

What is this?

A tool for taking interlinked notes (especially with LaTeX) on desktop and mobile devices.

Why NoteTeXer?

Note$\TeX$er is distinguished from other note-taking apps by two core principles:

  • Notes are webs, not binders! Notes should link to one another, forming a dense network of knowledge. So it's less like having a set of binders on the internet, and more like having your own personal mini-Wikipedia. Real subjects rarely organize neatly into closed subtopics, but rather have important connections throughout, and the structure of Note$\TeX$er represents this.
  • Notes are rich content! Notes are more than just text. The visual aspects of a note are important for triggering memory recall. Note$\TeX$er recognizes this, and provides a simple interface for styling your note, including images, rich-text formatting, and live-rendered $\LaTeX$ in-place for your beautiful mathematics. (Not to mention a suite of shortcuts for rapid $\LaTeX$ input, even on mobile devices!)

Who made this?

Hi, I'm Sam Bader, a soon-to-be PhD student in Engineering Physics, and I designed Note$\TeX$er as a resource for students in the math and science community. I started this project because I couldn't find any free tools out there which would let me build my own LaTeX-based quick-references, to have with me anywhere, on any device.

Hey, can I try?

Yes, literally right now!

If you want to try this out, go back to the home page and sign up (takes just a few seconds), and your account will automatically be populated by a series of notes that form a ten-minute tutorial on NoteTeXing efficiently.

The tool is provided free, as is, with no guarantees whatsoever. If you have suggestions, or would like to contribute to the project, talk to me at notetexer.help@gmail.com! If this tool becomes popular, and requires more space on the Parse server, then I may have make it donation-supported, but in the meantime, it's available at no cost, because I just love you.

Acknowledgements

Many thanks to these projects!

The software makes use of MathJax to live-render your LaTeX, jQuery Mobile for the smooth front-end, Parse for the simple backend, and Jquery Text Complete to auto-complete your LaTeX. Icons for the editor made by Icon Works and Freepik from Flaticon. The licenses for each of these wonderful tools can be found on their respective websites.
